Aluminum Anodizing and etching

First i made a solution of 1Mole NaOH(Lye crystal from the hardware store) to clean my aluminium part(40G NaOH into 1 Liter)
I soaked it in for about 2 minute and gave it a good rinse with tap water.
I sanded the surface and gave it a good rinse again.

Then i made a solution of 16%Sulfuric acid(I used red drain cleaner from hardware store) used a scrap piece of aluminium for the cathode(-) and connected my work piece(to be anodized) to the anode(+).
I used a computer power supply(10-12 volt was enough) and made myself a simple current regulator. Using This Calculator For my work piece i used 6 Amp/Ft2, 0.5 mils so that was 60 minute at 0.83 Amps
You can see the setup here:

You can see the result here before and after cleaning

Next i used some waxed newspaper(homehardware work well for me) paper to heat transfer some toner, used water to remove paper and used the same 1M NaOH solution(i first tryed ferric chloride but nothing happened) above to etch into the anodized layer.
This is the result once cleaned(I didnt take much time to make a good toner transfer, still the outcome is not too bad). Right side was sanded before anodizing:
